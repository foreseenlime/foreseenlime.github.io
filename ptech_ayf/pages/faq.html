Frequently asked questions

When is the next Auckland Youth Fest?

Auckland Youth Fest is happening on the 32nd of Decembuary.

What time does it start and finish?

Auckland Youth Fest goes from 8am to 5pm.

Are pass-outs available?

You can leave at any time, although you should check gate times in advance.

What are the gate opening times?

Gates are always open during the times that the festival is open.

What are the gate opening times?

Gates are always open during the times that the festival is open.

Where can I buy my tickets?

You can purchase tickets in our ticket store.

Where is my ticket?

When you buy a ticket, we send a copy of it to the email address you entered when buying tickets.

How do I share tickets with friends or family?

If you have the email for the tickets, you can send that to them so they can check in with it.

Do I need to print my ticket?

We recommend printing your tickets, but we do accept scanning them from your phone.

Who do I contact for more information about tickets?

You can email aklyouthfest@gmail.com, and your questions will be answered by one of our lovely support staff.

Can I ride my motorbike to Auckland Youth Fest?

Yes, just make sure to be careful for smaller people. If you travel by motorbike, you are not required to purchase a parking pass.

Can I bring my boat?

There isn't really anywhere to use it, but you can if you want to show it off. Boats will need a second parking pass, due to the extra space they take up.

Can I bring my camper vehicle or house truck?

You need a reserved camper vehicle site to bring a camper or house truck. Camper vehicles are not allowed in general car parks or camp grounds.

What is a parking pass?

Parking passes are required for vehicle entry into the festival car park. These must be purchased in advance. Vehicles with 3+ passengers get a partial refund at the gate, to encourage carpooling for sustainability.

Who needs a parking pass?

Every vehicle entering the festival must have a parking pass, except reserved camper vehicles and motorbikes.

I'm an artist / performer / volunteer / contractor / sponsor / crew-member / vendor, do I need a parking pass?

You may not need to purchase a parking pass. You will be informed if free parking is included on your ticket. We still encourage you to fill your vehicle with carpoolers to keep carbon emissions low.

Why should I purchase a parking pass?

This will reduce congestion and queueing times at gates. On-site passes cost more to cover handling fees. It is easier to process partial refunds for carpoolers than during payments on arrival. It helps festival organizers plan parking and traffic flow effectively.

Who can refund their parking passes?

Partial refunds will be issued to anyone who meets the following requirements: Standard motor vehicles with 3 or more passengers, 2-seaters with 2 passengers, fully electric vehicles with any number of passengers.

How to refund my parking pass?

Vehicles will be checked on arrival, where the holder of the parking pass will recieve credit to their wristband. They can use this credit in the festival, or exchange it for cash at the festival kiosk.

Can I bring alcohol to Auckland Youth Fest?

Alcohol is strictly prohibited, there will be searches at all entrances and during random site checks. Vendors offer a wide range of non-alcoholic and soft drinks, and free water.

Can I bring glass to Auckland Youth Fest?

Glass is permitted in the Auckland Showgrounds, although we advise you bring food or drinks in other containers.

Do I need to bring food and water?

You are welcome to bring your own food and water, but we strongly advise bringing it in reusable containers and bottles, as you can use free drinking water taps, and it prevents waste. If you don't want to, we have a huge variety of vendors who offer food and drinks.

Can I buy ice for coolers?

Yes, just ask for ice from one of our vendors.

Can I bring a BBQ?

No, gas cookers or fires are prohibited at the event.

Can I bring a pet?

Pets are allowed outside the Showgrounds as long as they are on a leash, but prohibited inside.

What is precycling?

Precycling is the practice of planning what you will bring to the festival, and stripping it to the essentials. Choose reusable containers, and bring everything back home. Attempt to precycle everything you bring, as we want to keep waste as low as possible.

What is a globelet?

A globelet is a reusable polyethylene cup for festivalgoers to use for drinks. The use of globelets prevents thousands of cups being sent to landfill, and you can keep them, and/or bring them to future festivals!
